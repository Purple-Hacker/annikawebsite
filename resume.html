Research Experience

Urry Lab

Present

Mapped galaxy dust distributions of 109 active galactic nuclei (black holes at the center of galaxies) with infrared and optical data from Hubble Space Telescope a year-long thesis with Professor Urry. Astronomical data calibration and reduction of FITS files accomplished in Python. When the research was complete, adapted my code into a publicly hosted GitHub software package so other astronomers could recreate my work and create attenuation (dust) maps of other galaxies as well. Presented final project at a senior thesis Mellon Forum. The Paul K. Richter and Evalyn E. Cook Richter Memorial Fund funded this research.

Newburgh Lab

September 2019 – August 2020

Created frequency versus intensity graphs of well-known bright stars on the Canadian Hydrogen Intensity Mapping Experiment, a telescope developed in 2018. I compared those graphs to theoretical graphs of the same star to find the statistical accuracy of the new telescope; found 3-5 frequency channels of 1024 over- and under-measuring intensity. Research done in Python, with packages scikit learn, astropy, and scipy, with aid of Cedar supercomputer time. The Paul K. Richter and Evalyn E. Cook Richter Memorial Fund funded this research.
